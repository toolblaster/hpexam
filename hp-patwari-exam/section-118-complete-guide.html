What is Section 118?

Section 118 of the Himachal Pradesh Tenancy and Land Reforms Act, 1972 is a protective legislation designed to preserve the limited agricultural land in the hilly terrain of Himachal Pradesh for the local agricultural community.

"No transfer of land including sales in execution of a decree of a civil court or for recovery of arrears of land revenues shall be valid in favour of a person who is not an agriculturist."

It places a restriction on buying land in Himachal Pradesh by Non-Agriculturists. It does not strictly ban "outsiders" based on domicile alone, but rather bans those who do not already own agricultural land in HP, preventing the alienation of scarce resources.

2

Who is an "Agriculturist"?

The definition is crucial for the exam. Under Section 2(2) of the Act:

Agriculturist

A landowner who:
  • Cultivates land personally in Himachal Pradesh.
  • Or owns land that is cultivated by tenants/laborers.
  • Key: Must already own agricultural land in HP.

Non-Agriculturist

Anyone who does not own agricultural land in HP.
  • Even a Bonafide Himachali (by birth) is a "Non-Agriculturist" for this Act if they don't own land.
  • Residents of other states.
  • Companies, Firms, Societies.

*Note: Generally, bonafide Himachalis have ancestral land, making them agriculturists. But the legal distinction is based on land ownership, not just domicile certificate.

3

The Prohibition & Exceptions

What is Banned?

Transfer of land by way of sale, gift, exchange, lease, mortgage with possession, or creation of tenancy is banned for Non-Agriculturists without permission.

Statutory Exceptions (No Permission Needed)

Transfer to a landless agricultural labourer (defined under the Act).
Transfer to State/Central Govt or Govt Companies.
Transfer by inheritance (Succession is generally allowed).
Purchase from HP Housing & Urban Development Authority (HIMUDA).
4

Permission Process (Rule 38A)

A non-agriculturist can buy land only with the prior permission of the State Government under Section 118(2)(h) read with Rule 38A of the HP Tenancy and Land Reforms Rules, 1975.

1. Purpose Application

Apply on Form LR-XIV. Purpose must be specified: Residential, Industrial, Hydropower, Religious, Charitable, etc.

2. Essential Documents

  • Tatima (Map): Showing the exact land to be bought.
  • Jamabandi: Proof of ownership of seller.
  • NOC: From TCP (Town & Country Planning) if in planning area.
  • Affidavit: Stating applicant has no other land in HP (for residential).
  • Project Report: For industrial/commercial use.

3. Granting Authority

The permission is granted by the State Government (Cabinet Level), though processed through the DC's office.

Industry / Hydropower As per Project Report Must be recommended by Dept of Industries.
Utilization Period 2 Years Land must be put to use within 2 years. DC can extend by 1 year.
6

Role of Patwari (Exam Focus)

The Patwari is the "eyes and ears" of the administration regarding Section 118.

  • Verification of Status: Patwari certifies if the buyer is an Agriculturist (based on records) or Non-Agriculturist.
  • Issuing Tatima: Must issue a fresh Tatima (map) for the land proposed to be sold.
  • Utilization Check: Patwari reports whether the land purchased with permission has been built upon within 2 years.

Benami Detection

Patwaris must be vigilant against Benami Transactions where a Non-Agriculturist buys land in the name of an Agriculturist to bypass Sec 118. Such transactions are void.

7

Violations & Vestment

What happens if the rules are broken?

Transaction Void

The sale deed is declared void ab initio.

State Vestment

The land (and any structure on it) vests in the State Government free from all encumbrances.

Penalties

Possible prosecution and eviction of the occupant.
